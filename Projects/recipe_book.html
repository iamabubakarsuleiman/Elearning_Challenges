Nigeria Jollof Rice

Ingredient Quantity
Rice 2 cups
Carrots (diced) 1/2 cup
Green peas 1/2 cup
Green beans (chopped) 1/2 cup
Sweet corn 1/4 cup
Bell peppers (diced) 1/2 cup
Chicken broth 1/2 cup
Oil 2 tbsp
Onion (chopped) 1 small
Garlic (minced) 2 cloves
Salt To taste
Curry powder 1 tsp
Thyme 1/2 tsp
Seasoning cubes 2-3

Instructions:

  1. Wash and parboil the rice for about 10 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a pot, add oil and sauté onions and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add diced carrots, green peas, green beans, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add the parboiled rice, curry powder, thyme, salt, and seasoning cubes.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, cover, and let it cook on low heat for about 15–20 minutes.
  6. Once the rice is cooked, add sweet corn and stir to combine. Serve hot.

Spaghetti Bolognese

Ingredient Quantity
Spaghetti 400g
Ground beef 500g
Onion (chopped) 1 large
Garlic (minced) 2 cloves
Carrot (diced) 1 medium
Celery (diced) 1 stalk
Tomatoes (blended) 4 large
Tomato paste 2 tbsp
Olive oil 2 tbsp
Beef or vegetable broth 1 cup
Italian seasoning 1 tsp
Salt To taste
Black pepper To taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant.
  3. Add the ground beef, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned, then drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the diced carrot and celery, stirring for 2–3 minutes until slightly softened.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste, blended tomatoes, and broth. Season with Italian seasoning, salt, and black pepper.
  6. Reduce heat and let the sauce simmer for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens.
  7. Serve the sauce over the spaghetti and enjoy.

Grilled Chicken Salad

Ingredient Quantity
Chicken breast 2 fillets
Olive oil 2 tbsp
Salt To taste
Black pepper To taste
Mixed salad greens 4 cups
Cherry tomatoes (halved) 1 cup
Cucumber (sliced) 1 medium
Red onion (thinly sliced) 1/4 medium
Avocado (sliced) 1 large
Feta cheese (crumbled) 1/4 cup
Balsamic vinaigrette or dressing of choice As desired

Instructions:

  1. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub the chicken breasts with olive oil, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Grill the chicken for 5–6 minutes on each side or until fully cooked. Let it rest for a few minutes, then slice thinly.
  4. In a large bowl, combine salad greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and avocado.
  5. Add the grilled chicken slices on top of the salad and sprinkle with crumbled feta cheese.
  6. Drizzle with balsamic vinaigrette or your favorite dressing, toss gently, and serve immediately.

Moimoi (Steamed Bean Pudding)

Ingredient Quantity
Black-eyed beans 2 cups
Red bell pepper 1 large
Onion 1 large
Ground crayfish 1 tbsp
Salt To taste
Seasoning cubes 1-2
Water As needed

Instructions:

  1. Soak the beans in water for a few hours, then remove the skins and blend with bell pepper and onion to form a smooth paste.
  2. Rub the chicken breasts with olive oil, salt, and black pepper.
  3. In a bowl, mix the bean paste with oil, crayfish, salt, and seasoning cubes. Add water gradually to reach a medium-thick consistency.
  4. Pour the mixture into small containers or leaves and steam in a pot with water for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until firm.
  5. Serve as a side dish or enjoy on its own.

Vegetable Soup (Nigerian Style)

Ingredient Quantity
Assorted meat 500g
Spinach or Ugu leaves 3 cups (chopped)
Onion 1 large
Palm oil 1/4 cup
Salt To taste
Pepper (ground) To taste
Ground crayfish 1 tbsp
Smenukfish 100g
Seasoning cubes 1-2
Water As needed

Instructions:

  1. Boil the assorted meat and smenukfish with onions, salt, and seasoning cubes until tender.
  2. In a pot, heat the palm oil and add the ground crayfish and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Add the cooked meat and smenuk, then pour in the chopped spinach or Ugu leaves.
  4. Stir well and let it cook for about 5–10 minutes, until the vegetables are softened but still vibrant.
  5. Adjust seasoning if needed, and serve with a side of rice, yam, or fufu.
